Non-Medical Caregiving Services

Our non-medical caregiving services provide compassionate, dignified support for elderly individuals and people with disabilities who need assistance with daily living activities. We connect families with professional, trained, and vetted caregivers throughout the United States who are committed to improving quality of life while maintaining independence and dignity.

Services We Provide

  • Activities of Daily Living (ADL): Personal hygiene, bathing, dressing, grooming
  • Meal Preparation & Nutrition: Shopping, cooking, meal planning, feeding assistance
  • Mobility & Transfers: Safe transfer assistance, ambulation support, physical mobility
  • Medication Reminders: Non-medical reminder support and tracking
  • Companionship & Social Support: Conversation, emotional support, engagement activities
  • Household Support: Light housekeeping, laundry, cleaning, organization
  • Transportation & Errands: Medical appointment transport, shopping, errands
  • Respite Care: Temporary relief for family caregivers
  • Behavioral Support: Compassionate care for dementia and cognitive conditions
  • Post-Recovery Assistance: Support during rehabilitation periods

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between non-medical and medical care?

Non-medical caregivers provide assistance with activities of daily living, companionship, and household support. Medical care requires nursing licenses. Our caregivers can remind clients to take medications but cannot administer injections or provide clinical care.

How are caregivers selected and trained?

Each caregiver undergoes comprehensive background checks, reference verification, skills assessment, and receives ongoing training in dementia care, mobility assistance, emergency response, and compassionate communication.

What scheduling options are available?

We offer flexible arrangements including hourly visits, daily care, overnight care, weekend support, and 24/7 live-in caregiving. Schedules can be adjusted as needs change.

How do you ensure quality and reliability?

We have dedicated care coordinators who monitor satisfaction, provide backup caregivers for emergencies, maintain regular communication with families, and have 24/7 support available.

What areas do you serve?

We provide non-medical caregiving services throughout the United States. Contact us for availability in your specific area.

How do I get started?

Contact us for a free consultation. We'll discuss your needs, answer questions, and explain the matching process. There's no obligation to proceed.
